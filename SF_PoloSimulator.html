Read this before you trust it. Your figures: vehicles, acquisition, book, financed amount, rental, deposit, term, and that the bond is served from rental income while the mine compounds. Your cost stack: bond on R310,000, insurance at 4% a year, tracker at R250 a month. Maintenance and admin start at zero because you did not name them, and both flag below. Placeholders I chose: funding rate, default rate, residual, resale discount. The output is only as good as those. Insurance is charged on the declining sum insured, not flat on R310,000, which is how comprehensive motor cover actually prices. That makes it cheaper over time, and is the friendlier reading. The bond is a level amortising payment across the term. No fees, covenants or tax modelled. Required income assumes a lender's usual one third of net pay rule.
